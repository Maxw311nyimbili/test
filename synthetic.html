What is Synthetic Biology

Synthetic biology is a multidisciplinary field of research that seeks to create or redesign biological systems from the smallest constituent parts possible, such as DNA, proteins, and other organic molecules. Synthetic biology applies engineering principles such as standardization, modularity, and abstraction to genetic manipulation, viewing genes and other DNA features as "parts" and cells as "molecular machines.

Techniques used in Synthetic Biology

Techniques used in synthetic biology include gene splicing, metabolic engineering and directed evolution. Furthermore, industrial biotech scientists and other biotech companies have been utilizing theses forms of synthetic biology for years.

Applications of Synthetic Biology

Synthetic biology has the potential to advance various applications, such as nutrition, safety, and manufacturing, by expanding the breadth of biology from existing and natural systems to non-natural and viable systems. These applications can be extended to the medical field in assisting with finding ways to effects elimainate diseases including but not limited to cancer, coronary heart diseases, diabetes, as well as Alzheimer's disease
